What is Machine Learning?

Machine learning (ML) is a subset of AI that consists of algorithms that reflect how humans learn. Initially, when these programs are written, they are very inaccurate and have many flaws, but over a prolonged time, the technology becomes faster and more accurate. (Hosch, 2024)

  1. Decision
  2. Error
  3. Optimisation

After executing the algorithm, the machine can identify the error made and adjust and find an alternative solution to the problem. By repeating this process, the technology becomes faster and more effective over time, becoming an invaluable tool for businesses. (Stryker & Kavlakoglu, 2024)

What are the typical uses for Machine Learning?

ML has many uses in modern-day technology, some more commonly known than others. A primary example is the ever-improving biometric systems. Many Apple and Android devices use biometric scanners, such as fingerprint scans and facial recognition, which were added to Apple Devices towards the end of 2017. (Cipriani, 2020). Biometric systems use ML to observe patterns within the biometric scan. When fingerprint scans are initially set up, the biometric system scans for unique patterns in the fingerprint and uses algorithms to unlock only when that specific pattern is detected. (Reddy et al., 2023)

Another common example is product recommendations on retail websites, such as Amazon and online retailers. It uses ML to observe the users purchases, clicks and search history. The algorithm then filters the information to recommend products based on what it has observed about the user. Many different algorithms are used for this, but Collaborative Filtering is most commonly used. (NVIDIA, 2021)
